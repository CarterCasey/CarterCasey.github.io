Network Layer

Forwarding (IP)

  • When packet reaches router, make longest prefix match on destination.
  • Match is done against table, holds IPs and ports.

Routing Protocols (BGP, OSPF)

  • Hierarchical structure in routers:
    • Customer-Provider Relationship between normal router and ISP.
    • Peering: Between large ISPs (could be tier 1, but also smaller).
  • Provider tries to advertise prefix of customer to everyone.
  • Provider also tries to advertise peer prefixes to customers.
  • A peer may advertise to peer, but usually not (doesn't make money).

Border Gateway Protocol (BGP)

  • Operates over TCP
  • Announcement:
    • Prefix
    • Next-hop (IP adress of next router to accept the prefix)
    • AS Path (Autonomous System numbers, all along the way)

Open Shortest Path First (OSPF)

  • Based on Dijkstra's
  • Weights are based either on number of hops or ...
  • Internal BGP tells you which router to go to - OSPF tells you how to get there.
