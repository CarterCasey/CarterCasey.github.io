Wi-fi vs Ethernet (CSMA/CA vs CSMA/CD)

  • Both start with carrier sensing.

  • NOTE: Wi-fi has the same kind of carrier sensing that ethernet does.

  • Wi-fi, instead of starting immediately, the transmission is sent after DIFS.

  • After transmission in Wi-fi, wait SIFS and send ack back to transmitter.
  • SIFS is shorter than DIFS, so that we can prioritize acks.
  • After transmission wait DIFS + random backoff before sending new transmission.
  • The gaps aren't necessary in ethernet because it instantly detects collision and stops.

RTS/CTS

  • Request to send, clear to send.
  • We have several access points on the ends, and one in the middle that's just outside their range.
  • CTS contains length of transmission. Allows "Virtual Carrier Sensing."
