The name of the course

Internet-Scale
Basically, global. There are a lot of things to think about when we see how large the world is, from latency times and consistent delivery, to international laws and social implications.
For that matter, systems this large should be simple. If you want billions of people to agree to use something, you better hope a decent fraction of them understand it.

Distributed Systems
A system that has many computing components that work together and communicate with each other. Technically, a multi-core machine is a distributed system.

Tim Berners-Lee

  • Grew up with computers - his parents met working on Harvard Mark 1.
  • Created Web to help colleagues at CERN communicate, share documents between differently designed computers. While building, he kept in mind what's needed to make it world wide.
  • World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) Group to pull together all the ideas and components on the web.
