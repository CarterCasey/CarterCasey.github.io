Internet Architecture

Circuit Switching

The data is sent through one dedicated path, continuously.

  • Good (necessary) for analog.
  • Nice for continuous stream signal.
  • All paths are predictable and continuous, so we can optimize on the switches.

Packet Switching

Each packet of a message finds an independent route through the network.

  • Digital lets this happen.
  • When we're dealing with lots of small/medium messages, this is more adaptable.
  • Makes the network less important than what goes through it. Can't predict the path, so why bother making switches smart.

End-to-End Arguments

If we need to do something (provide a functionality) at an application level, trying to add pieces of the functionality into the lower levels of the system may be redundant and cause a performance loss.

TCP as End-to-End Protocol

  • Doesn't bother forcing all packets to be in order during transmission.
  • Only tries to re-get packet once it reaches target, not in the middle of transmission.

Significance of End-to-End Argument

  • It's a good way to start approaching systems that are prone to errors.
  • An added argument for net neutrality.
    • We shouldn't have to worry about the network, just what comes through it from one end to another.
    • Tim BL didn't have to ask permission to make the web. He didn't have to fight against the ISPs to get his ideas out there.

Errors and Error Recovery

Note: Solar/cosmic radiation causes the most bit errors in modern memory.

Errors happen, it's unavoidable. How do we deal with them?

  • Try to recover from the error.
  • Tell a higher level to recover from the error.
  • Just stop running if there's an error. (Fail Stop)
