Compilers - Lecture 15

Mar 30

Optimization

  • Big ticket items are instruction selection and register allocation.

Nested Functions

  • Brings up whole hosts of problems.
  • You might want to refer to another function's activation record.
    • This is fine with non-functional languages - just refer back to record.
    • But with first class functions, you have to refer to that activation record even after the call to the outer function has finished.
  • We make persistent activation records.

Target Machines

  • RISC gives us easier machine code to work with.
  • We want to walk through AST to find pieces that can be done with simple instructions.
  • On the other hand, x64 has more powerful instructions.

  • To work with those larger instructions, we want to "tile" the AST.

  • Best system is Bottom-Up Rewrite Generator (BURG).
