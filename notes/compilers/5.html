Compilers - Lecture 5

Feb 11

The lexer has "clunky syntax."

Basically:

  • Regex on the left, action on the right
  • Also get to have states. Can transition into another state from action

C++ is a bigger mess to lex than C, unsurprisingly.

Context-Free Grammars

  • Spec for scanner is in regexes. Linear time.
  • Spec for parser is in context-free grammars. O(n^3).
  • Try to avoid n^3 times, look for restricted context-free grammars that have linear time solutions.
  • Early's algorithm for solving these uses dynamic programming.
  • GLR is one of these.

Why regexes aren't powerful enough

  • Can't count arbitrarily.
  • Matching parens, for instance, won't work.

Making Context-Free Grammars

  • Terminal Symbols - tokens
  • Non-Terminal Symbols
  • Form Productions, which are: NT -> (T ⋃ NT)*
  • Use Backus-Naur Form to write down CFG
