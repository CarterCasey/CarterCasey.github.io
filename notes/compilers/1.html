Compilers - Lecture 1

Jan 14

Book: Cooper & Torczon - "Engineering a Compiler"

Goal: Build a compiler. Walk through the process of compiling, solving the problems that arise in ML.

Trying to compile an imperative C-ish language (with a little OO) to x64.

No exams(!)

What should a compiler do?

  • Be correct.
  • Optimize.
  • Catch errors.

How do we ensure correctness?

  • Build test suites.
  • Prove it.
  • Use syntax directed compilation.
  • Make test generation tools.
  • Generate pieces of the compiler in ways that follow a spec.

What does a compiler need to know?

  • Structure of the language.
  • What can't/shouldn't be done in a language.
  • ABI.

Pieces of Compiler:

  • Lexer/Scanner - takes ascii text source code, produces tokens.
  • (Grammar) Parser - takes tokens and organizes into grammatical structure (AST).
  • Semantic Checks (types, whether objects exist, contol flow).
  • Code Generator - take AST and produce assembly code.

Optimization can happen at two levels - high level optimization in the actual AST, low level optimization in the assembly itself.
